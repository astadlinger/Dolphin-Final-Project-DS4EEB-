#### How do predator presence and vegetation cover affect rodent foraging success?
The rationale behind this is that apex predator presence and varying habitat types will cause a difference in seed foraging rates. Wood mice are heavily reliant on coverage for foraging, likely resulting in more seed predation in those microhabitats. Top predator presence will result in a mesopredator population decrease due to predation. This reduction in mesopredators such as Red Foxes, Egyptian Mongooses, and Badgers will result in less pressure on Wood Mice, inherently causing more foraging success. 
Iberian Lynx reintroduction was an expensive and risky conservation strategy, so measuring the success and effects of its presence its crittical when it comes to the future management of these ecosystems and reintroduction efforts across other unbalanced ecosystems. *****GONNA REVISE*****
